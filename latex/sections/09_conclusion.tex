\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

CircuitCraft embeds a real modified nodal analysis solver, complete with
trapezoidal-integration reactive-element models, a charge-controlled memristor, a linearized
diode, and an ideal op-amp modeled as a nullor, within an unmodified copy of Minecraft's
ordinary block-placing and wiring mechanics. Every voltage, current, and internal component
state observed by a player is produced by actually solving the circuit that player
constructed, rather than by a scripted stand-in for one, down to details -- an explicit,
player-placed ground reference; sources that must be switched on before they contribute
current; a multi-channel oscilloscope permitting several points within the same circuit to
be compared at once, alongside a second, X-Y probe for comparing two signals directly against
each other, and a third, complex-valued AC solver sharing the same wiring topology that lets a
dedicated source be swept across a configurable frequency range and read out as a Bode plot,
complete with a two-pole gain model giving the op-amp the frequency-dependent rolloff an
ideal, infinite-bandwidth nullor could never itself exhibit -- chosen specifically to render ideas that are usually assumed (a reference node,
a source being ``off'') as objects a player must construct and reason about independently. The mod and its source code, together with this
paper's typeset source, are released under permissive licenses; the author welcomes
contributions, in particular toward the persisted regression suite and the classroom
evaluation described in Section~\ref{sec:limitations}.
